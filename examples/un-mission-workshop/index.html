Alignment tool.

// decision-making
02

Decision-making tool.

We make decisions all day. The mission gives the next step when policy doesn't cover it. Disney's street sweeper sees a child crying. Puts down the broom. Goes to deliver magic, fun, and happiness.

Do the right thing tool.

Organizations rooted in this start doing the right thing – for everyone, for the community, for the planet. Without being mandated to. They recycle. They change the lightbulb. They extend "everyone" to mean everyone.

04
A quick clear-up

What it isn't.

// two common confusions
Not this
// slogan vs mission
01

Not a slogan or tagline.

Slogans are external. "Just Do It." That's marketing. Mission is internal. The secret sauce. We don't care if anyone outside knows what "leave more Remi Moments" means – we know.

// remi moments. internal language. theirs alone.
Not this either
// happiness vs mattering
02

Not a make-everyone-happy tool.

Bad news still gets delivered. People don't always leave happy. The mission isn't about happiness – it's about whether people are left feeling that they matter, even when the answer is no.

// my daughter wants more youtube. i say no. she's mad. the commitment holds.

Part five

Your turn.

// how do we find ours
Start here
// three prompts

How do you want to leave people?

  • 01 / Everyone you come into contact with – clients, employees, even people who've left. How do you want them to feel?
  • 02 / Your closest, dearest person – after a lunch together. How would you want to leave them, such that you could "shoot me now" and be okay?
  • 03 / Your children – if they were left this way in life, would you be content?
What we've already lived
// before, during, after. find the stories.

When have you lived this?

  • Stories of when you showed up big.
  • Times when someone felt massively supported.
  • Moments where the work transformed someone.
From your DNA
// we didn't start from nothing.

What's worth carrying forward?

  • Phrases or values from your past you still believe in.
  • Commitments or ways of being you've liked.
  • Pieces of your culture that deserve to come along.

  • Words and phrases coming up again and again.
