SECTION 01 / POSITION

What the brand holds.

NBC sits between two ideas that usually pull apart: fresh, current, AI-fluent, build-first, and grounded, human, steady, care-led. Most consultants pick one. NBC is both, and the visual system is built to hold both.

The audience is mission-driven leadership teams in healthcare, manufacturing, housing, hospitality, and creative sectors, with an increasingly important audience of younger founder-led companies.

Voice

Five traits define the voice: clear, human, practical, thoughtful, steady. Speak like a trusted advisor, not a vendor. Use plain language with depth behind it. Concrete nouns over abstractions: teams, decisions, systems, meetings, plans, roles, tools.

SECTION 02 / COLOR

The palette.

White is the default surface for everything digital. Ink is the contrast variant - used for decks projected in dim rooms or accent sections within a light-led page. Off-white is for documents only. Red is punctuation, never paint, except for one designated full-bleed surface per artifact (typically the closing slide).

SECTION 05 / SIGNATURE MOVES

Four graphic moves.

What makes a surface feel like NBC. Use all of them across the system.

The red dot

The brand fingerprint. Lives next to "CONSULTING" on the wordmark, before eyebrow labels on slides, and as a divider in select contexts.

Racing numbers

On section break slides, the section number appears as an oversized character in brand red at reduced opacity (35% on light, 25% on dark), sitting partially behind the headline word. Inspired by racing numbers on rally cars. The number can bleed off the right edge.

SECTION 07 / WHAT THIS IS NOT

To stay honest.

This brand is not a serif/literary brand. Not a warm cream-led editorial brand (earlier explorations used cream as a primary surface; the brand now uses pure white as default with off-white reserved for documents). Not a dark-led brand (earlier explorations defaulted to ink; the brand now defaults light with dark as a deliberate contrast variant). Not a corporate consultancy aesthetic (no chevrons, drop shadows, stock people-laughing-around-laptops). Not a startup SaaS aesthetic (no purple gradients, glass-morphism, AI swoosh graphics). Not a coach/personal-development aesthetic (no soft pastels, no "unlock your potential" register).
